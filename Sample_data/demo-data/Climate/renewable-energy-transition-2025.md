# Global Renewable Energy Transition 2025

**Author:** International Energy Outlook Group
**Category:** Climate

## Overview

Renewable capacity additions set a record in 2024, with solar and wind accounting for over 90% of new generation capacity worldwide. This report examines cost trends, grid integration, and storage.

## Cost Trends

The levelized cost of utility-scale solar fell another 12% year over year, remaining the cheapest source of new electricity in most markets. Onshore wind costs stabilized while offshore wind faced supply-chain-driven cost pressure.

## Grid Integration

Curtailment of renewable output rose in regions where transmission buildout lagged generation. Flexible demand, interconnection, and storage were the three most cited levers for absorbing higher renewable penetration.

## Energy Storage

Battery storage deployments doubled, driven by falling lithium-ion prices and grid-scale projects. Four-hour storage became economically competitive with gas peaker plants in several high-solar markets.
