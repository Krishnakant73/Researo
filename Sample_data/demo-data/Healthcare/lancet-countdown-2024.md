# The Lancet Countdown on Health and Climate 2024

**Author:** The Lancet Countdown
**Category:** Healthcare

## Introduction

The Lancet Countdown on health and climate change tracks the health impacts of climate change and the health co-benefits of climate action across 47 indicators.

## Heat and Health

Heatwave-related deaths among people over 65 years old rose by 85% versus the 1990s baseline, dominating direct-mortality attributions to climate change. Labor capacity losses from extreme heat reached an estimated 512 billion potential work hours in 2023.

## Food Security

Changing temperature and rainfall patterns are increasingly threatening food security, with net crop yield losses of 6% attributable to climate change since 1980. The duration of the growth season for major cereals shortened in most tropical regions.

## Health Co-benefits

Rapid decarbonisation produces immediate health benefits from reduced air pollution — an estimated 460,000 deaths per year would be avoided at 1.5°C stabilisation. Active transport and dietary shifts add further reductions in non-communicable disease burden.
