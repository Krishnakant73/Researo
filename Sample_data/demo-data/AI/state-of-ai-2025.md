# State of AI Report 2025

**Author:** AI Index Steering Committee
**Category:** AI

## Executive Summary

Enterprise adoption of AI agents accelerated sharply in 2024–2025. Model quality, tool-use reliability, and cost per token improved together, unlocking use cases that had been unattractive in prior years. This report synthesizes survey data from 1,200 organizations across 34 countries.

## Enterprise Adoption

Enterprise adoption of agentic workflows grew 218% year over year, with 62% of surveyed companies piloting at least one autonomous agent in production. Regulated industries lagged the median, citing auditability and access-control concerns. Financial services and healthcare reported the slowest rollout, while software and media moved fastest.

## Productivity Gains

In structured knowledge tasks — coding, research, and customer support — agent-augmented workers completed comparable tasks in 35–47% less time. Quality did not degrade for tasks where independent validators reviewed outputs. The largest gains appeared in first-draft generation and information synthesis rather than final decision-making.

## Risks

The primary risks cited by CIOs are cost overruns, security posture, and inability to audit agent decision paths. Cost is the number-one concern in mid-market companies; audit is the number-one concern in regulated industries. 41% of respondents reported at least one incident where an agent took an unintended action.

## Governance

Only 24% of firms report formal audit trails for agent decisions. Structured telemetry — tokens, tool calls, latency, cost, retries — is emerging as the minimum bar for governance. Organizations with mature observability practices reported 3x higher confidence in expanding agent deployments.
