# Cloud Infrastructure Trends 2025

**Author:** Distributed Systems Review
**Category:** Technology

## Overview

Cloud spending continued to shift toward AI-optimized infrastructure in 2025. This report examines GPU availability, serverless adoption, and the economics of running inference at scale.

## Accelerated Compute

Demand for GPU and custom-silicon capacity outpaced supply, pushing providers toward reserved-capacity models and multi-year commitments. Inference now accounts for a growing share of accelerated-compute spend relative to training.

## Serverless and Edge

Serverless adoption expanded beyond event-driven workloads into steady-state services as cold-start latency improved. Edge deployment gained traction for latency-sensitive inference, though tooling and observability lagged centralized cloud.

## Cost Optimization

FinOps practices matured, with organizations reporting 20–30% savings from rightsizing, spot capacity, and workload scheduling. The largest inefficiencies were idle GPU reservations and over-provisioned inference endpoints.
