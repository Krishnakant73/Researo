# Global Data Privacy Compliance Review 2024

**Author:** Privacy and Data Protection Forum
**Category:** Legal

## Summary

More than 70% of the world's population is now covered by some form of data protection law. This review examines convergence and divergence across major regimes including GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and newer frameworks in Asia and Latin America.

## Consent and Legal Basis

Regulators increasingly scrutinize consent mechanisms, penalizing dark patterns and pre-checked boxes. Legitimate interest as a legal basis faces tighter documentation and balancing-test requirements.

## Cross-Border Transfers

Cross-border data transfers remain a compliance hotspot following successive invalidations of transfer frameworks. Standard contractual clauses and transfer impact assessments became standard practice for multinational data flows.

## Enforcement Trends

Aggregate fines under GDPR surpassed €4.5 billion cumulatively. Enforcement shifted toward systemic issues — inadequate security, excessive retention, and opaque profiling — rather than isolated breaches.
