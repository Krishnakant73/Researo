# AI Governance and Regulation Landscape 2025

**Author:** Center for Technology Policy
**Category:** Legal

## Overview

Regulatory frameworks for artificial intelligence matured rapidly in 2024–2025. This report compares the EU AI Act, emerging US state laws, and voluntary industry commitments.

## Risk-Based Frameworks

The EU AI Act classifies systems into unacceptable, high, limited, and minimal risk tiers, with obligations scaling accordingly. High-risk systems must meet requirements for data governance, transparency, human oversight, and post-market monitoring.

## Transparency and Disclosure

Multiple jurisdictions introduced obligations to label AI-generated content and disclose when users interact with an automated system. Enforcement mechanisms and penalty structures vary widely, creating compliance complexity for multinational deployments.

## Liability and Accountability

Questions of liability for autonomous agent actions remain unsettled. Proposed approaches range from strict-liability regimes on deployers to shared responsibility between model providers and integrators. Auditability of decision paths is a recurring requirement across draft laws.
